Biggest route optimization: The Southwest Loop

Original route: Zion → Bryce → SLC → Bonneville → Canyonlands → Arches → Grand Junction → Aspen → then back to Grand Canyon and Monument Valley. That's two major direction reversals.

Optimized: Las Vegas → Havasu Falls → Grand Canyon → Sedona → Monument Valley → Four Corners → Mesa Verde → Antelope Canyon → Zion → Bryce → Capitol Reef → Canyonlands → Arches → Aspen → Denver. Saves ~400 miles and hours of wasted driving.

Why the mileage is higher than your spreadsheet (14,645 mi)

The spreadsheet total skipped some legs and had route inefficiencies that, when corrected, add back miles on net. The optimized route also adds recommended stops (Lassen Volcanic, Valley of Fire, Columbia River Gorge, Peyto Lake). The result is more total miles but significantly fewer wasted driving hours — and a much more logical geographic flow.

Ideal timing: Late May → September (East to West to Southwest)

Start in Maine (Acadia) in late May when the Precipice Trail reopens and lobster season peaks. Hit Glacier NP by early July before peak crowds. Reach the Southwest by September for cooler temps and far better permit odds. Wrap the East Coast loop in October with Appalachian fall foliage. Avoid Death Valley June–August (110°F+) and Crater Lake's rim before July (snow-covered).
